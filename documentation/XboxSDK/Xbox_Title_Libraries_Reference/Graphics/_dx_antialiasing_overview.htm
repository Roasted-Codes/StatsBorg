Antialiasing

Antialiasing is a technique you can use to reduce the appearance of stair-step pixels when drawing any line that is not exactly horizontal or vertical. In three-dimensional scenes, this artifact is most noticable on the boundaries between polygons of different colors. Antialiasing effectively blends the pixels at the boundaries to produce a more natural look to the scene.

The Xbox supports two antialiasing techniques: edge antialiasing and full-surface antialiasing. Full-surface antialiasing can be accomplished by using either multisampling or supersampling. The technique that is best for your title depends on your requirements for performance and visual fidelity.
