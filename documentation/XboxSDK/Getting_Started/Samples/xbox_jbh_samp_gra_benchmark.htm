Benchmark Sample

The Benchmark sample renders a large number of polygons under various conditions to benchmark the performance of the system. A large number of polygons are rendered and various options can be altered, such as the number of textures and lights.

Overview

The Benchmark sample draws 25 pairs of ribbons, each consisting of two long meshes of triangles superimposed on one another. The ribbons are identical, except that the normals are opposite between them, and the culling is switched as well. This causes the outside of one ribbon to be drawn, and then the inside of the other, producing what looks like a seamless corkscrew of ribbon.

GPU Performance

The Benchmark sample was included because it provides a tool that will let you evaluate the performance impact of using various rendering attributes such as textures, directional lights, spotlights, point lights, triangle strips versus triangle lists meshes, and system memory vertex buffers versus video memory vertex buffers.

In the Benchmark sample, triangle strips perform better than triangle lists. However, the relative performance of triangle strips versus triangle lists can vary from mesh to mesh. This depends on several factors, including how well suited the topology of the mesh is to triangle strips and how well the vertices of the mesh can be internally cached by the graphics chip. Do not extrapolate the Benchmark sample results and assume that triangle strips are always faster than triangle lists—it depends on the data. You should run tests with your own meshes.

Adding or Removing Lights

You can add or remove lights by holding down the appropriate button (A, B, or Black, depending on light type) and pressing up or down on the dpad.

Controls

For a complete list of controller mappings, press the Back button on the Xbox controller while this sample is running.
