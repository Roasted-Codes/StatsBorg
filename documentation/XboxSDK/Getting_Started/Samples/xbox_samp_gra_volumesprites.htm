VolumeSprites Sample

The VolumeSprites sample displays a system of particles, or point sprites, and applies texels to them taken from a 3D volume texture. Normally, a texture is thought of as a 2D image that has a width and a height and whose texels are addressed with two coordinates, tu and tv. Volume textures are the 3D counterparts, with a width, height, and depth, and they are addressed with three coordinates, tu, tv, and tw.

Volume textures can be used for interesting effects, such as patchy fog, explosions, and so on.

Overview

Volume textures are 3D collections of pixels (texels) that can be used to paint a 2D primitive such as a triangle or a line. Three-element texture coordinates are required for each vertex of a primitive that is to be textured with a volume. As the primitive is drawn, each contained pixel is filled with the color value from some pixel within the volume, in accordance with rules analogous to the 2D texture case. Volumes are not rendered directly because there are no 3D primitives that can be painted with them.

Remarks

This sample renders a group of particles onto the screen and applies texels from a 3D texture. As a result, the particles change color as their changing x,y,z locations map to different tu,tv,tw locations in the volume texture.

Note that using 3D textures can reduce the fill-rate potential by as much as 50 percent over using 2D textures.
