ProjectedTexture Sample

The ProjectedTexture sample demonstrates the use of projected textures. With this technique, a 2D texture can be "projected" on to a 3D object, much like a film projector casts an image on to a wall. Projected textures can be used for a variety of effects, such as shadows and lighting.

Overview

The ProjectedTexture sample projects a "spotlight" texture onto a cube. The texture is projected perspective-corrected. In this sample, a cube is rendered with the D3DTTFF_PROJECTED flag so that Direct3D generates the texture coordinates. The texture coordinates are 3D and are transformed into place via the texture transform (which is updated with each frame as objects are moved).

Note   A limitation of projected textures is that the texture appears on any face with which the projection intersects. So the texture will also appear on the faces of the object that lie on the opposite side of the projection source.

Controls
