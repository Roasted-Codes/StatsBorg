VolumeTexture Sample

The VolumeTexture sample illustrates how to use the new volume textures in Direct3D. Normally, a texture is thought of as a 2D image that has a width and a height and whose "texels" are addressed with two coordinates, tu and tv. Volume textures are the 3D counterparts, with a width, height, and depth, and they are addressed with three coordinates, tu, tv, and tw.

Volume textures can be used for interesting effects, such as patchy fog, explosions, and so on.

Overview

Volume textures are no more difficult to use than 2D textures. In this sample source code, look for the vertex declaration (which has a third texture coordinate), texture creation (which also takes a depth dimension), and texture locking (also with the third dimension). The 3D rasterizer interpolates texel values-much as it would for 2D textures.
