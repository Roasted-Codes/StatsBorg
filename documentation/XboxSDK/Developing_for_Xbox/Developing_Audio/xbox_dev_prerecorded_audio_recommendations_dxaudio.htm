Prerecorded Audio Recommendations

For prerecorded musical scores and ambience, we recommend delivering two versions of your audio content:

  • 48-kHz PCM wave with six discrete channels (for 5.1 playback)
  • 48-kHz PCM wave with two discrete channels (for stereo playback), optionally matrix encoded (for 4.0 playback)

If you want to deliver prerecorded audio in compressed form, remember that each hardware buffer can be routed to one or more specific output channels as you see fit. For instance, you could create three stereo WMA or ADPCM streams (which would be six discrete channels of audio) and map them to 5.1 channels in hardware. While ADPCM data is decoded in the hardware, WMA data is decoded in the software, so there will be a CPU hit for using WMA streams and you could have bandwidth issues if you don't position the audio streams close to each other on the DVD or hard disk.

If you wish to deliver only one version of your audio content, the six-channel mix can be mixed down within the hardware for playback on stereo and matrix (4.0) systems. However, keep in mind that some information may be altered or lost. For example:

  • Existing LFE data is ignored and the low frequency channel is synthesized from the left and right signals
  • Discrete left and right surround channels are merged into a single mono surround
  • Nothing will be sent to the center channel unless it is explicitly addressed to the center

For real-time, 3D positioned audio, mono waves are required.
