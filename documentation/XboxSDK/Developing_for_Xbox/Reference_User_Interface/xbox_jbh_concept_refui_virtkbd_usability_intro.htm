Usability Tests

Participants

These designs were usability tested over the course of several usability sessions with console gamers in labs at Microsoft Corporation as well as internationally. The gamers were mainly male and ranged in age from 14-30.

Apparatus

Participants used an Interact Hammerhead controller to provide input during these usability tests. The controller was the same as the one that was shipped with the Xbox Development Kits (XDKs). Participants viewed the prototypes on either a 17-inch computer monitor with a resolution of 640 x 480 or a 25-inch Sony Trinitron television that was showing an NTSC-encoded computer video signal at 640 x 480 pixel resolution.

Method

Participants entered typical game information with many different Microsoft® Visual Basic® and Macromedia Director prototypes. The information entered by the user ranged from initials to online usernames to registration information. The time necessary to complete the input was recorded for each trial. The order in which the prototypes were used by the participants was different for each person. After finishing the tasks with each keyboard, participants completed the Post System Study Usability Questionnaire (PSSUQ), which consists of 19 items and breaks usability of the product down into four factors: overall usability, system usefulness, information quality, and interface quality.
